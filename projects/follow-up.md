<p class="dropcap">
  AVE-RD's first wave moved <span class="font-osf">600</span> schools and
  <span class="font-osf">43,000</span> students. The follow-up wave moves
  <em>time</em>: a decade later, we re-survey the same cohort and link
  them to the Dominican social-security earnings panel. The follow-up is
  open to foundation and bilateral funders. This page is the ask.
</p>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">What funding unlocks</div>
  <h2>Four budget lines, four deliverables</h2>
</div>

<ol class="numbered-list reveal">
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>Cohort re-survey &middot; <span class="font-osf">n</span> households</h3>
      <p>
        Mixed-mode (CATI + in-person) instrument fielded across all
        <span class="font-osf">18</span> educational regions. Includes
        contact-tracing protocol for migrants, who account for an
        estimated <span class="font-osf">m</span>% of the
        cohort.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>Administrative-data linkage</h3>
      <p>
        AVE-RD panel cross-walked to the Tesorer&iacute;a de la Seguridad
        Social earnings records. IDEICE is the data steward; budget
        covers the legal, IRB, and infrastructure costs of the linkage,
        plus de-identification for the open replication kit.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>Open replication kit &amp; long-run working paper</h3>
      <p>
        Cleans, packages, and releases the de-identified panel and
        Stata pipeline. The pre-analysis plan for the long-run wave is
        already registered.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>The brief series</h3>
      <p>
        Three two-page briefs (<a href="/briefs/what-worked/">researchers</a>,
        <a href="/briefs/why-follow-up/">funders</a>,
        <a href="/briefs/for-ministers/">ministries</a>) plus
        translation into Spanish, Portuguese, and French.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
</ol>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">Who we are talking to</div>
  <h2>Funders we are well-matched with</h2>
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Foundations focused on Latin America &amp; the Caribbean</strong> with an education or human-capital portfolio.</li>
  <li><strong>Bilateral donors</strong> (USAID and peer agencies) extending or building on the founding pilot.</li>
  <li><strong>Multilaterals</strong> (IDB, World Bank, CAF) with operating programs in Dominican basic education.</li>
  <li><strong>University-housed funds</strong> at our PIs' institutions willing to second-fund individual budget lines.</li>
</ul>

<div class="signal-panel signal-panel--research reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow">Pre-existing commitments</div>
  <p>
    <strong>Institutional.</strong> MINERD funds the in-school operation
    out of the DOP base budget. IDEICE is the data partner. J-PAL LAC
    underwrites the country-team coordination. These commitments mean a
    funder's marginal dollar buys research and replication, not field
    setup.
  </p>
  <p>
    <strong>Methodological.</strong> The pre-analysis plan for the
    long-run outcomes is registered. Effect sizes from the first wave
    are published. The follow-up does not depend on a new identification
    strategy &mdash; only on funding the field operation that converts
    the existing design into earnings outcomes.
  </p>
</div>
