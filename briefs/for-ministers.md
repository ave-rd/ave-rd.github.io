<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">Why this brief exists</div>
  <h2>The decision in front of a minister</h2>
</div>

<p>
  AVE-RD's intervention is unusually portable: four short videos, a
  classroom protocol, a survey instrument, and a discussion guide. The
  inputs the program needs from a country are well-defined &mdash; a
  recent wage-by-schooling-level dataset, a list of public schools in
  grades 7&ndash;12, and a regional coordinator network inside the
  ministry. This brief lays out what adapting AVE to a new country looks
  like in practice: the cost order, the partnership model, and the
  six-month launch sequence.
</p>

<div class="signal-panel signal-panel--research reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow">Existence proof: Peru</div>
  <p>
    The closest analogue is <strong>Decidiendo para un Futuro Mejor
    (DFM)</strong> in Peru &mdash; same intervention design, funded by
    the US Department of Labor, managed by IPA and J-PAL with MINEDU
    Per&uacute;'s MineduLAB. DFM reduced school dropout by
    <span class="font-osf">18.8</span>% in urban areas at a marginal
    cost of <span class="font-osf">$0.05</span> per student and was
    subsequently scaled to all
    <span class="font-osf">2,001</span> Peruvian secondary schools
    with full class days. The DFM concept note, results brief, and
    instruments are all in the public domain &mdash; see the
    <a href="/projects/sister-projects/">sister-projects page</a> for
    the full link set. Where AVE has been done, this is what the
    next minister has to read.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">Partnership model</div>
  <h2>Who does what</h2>
</div>

<ol class="numbered-list reveal">
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>The ministry owns delivery</h3>
      <p>
        A ministry's orientation/psychology directorate (or equivalent)
        runs the in-school screening, regional coordination, and
        implementation evidence. The intervention is, fundamentally, a
        ministry program with research instrumentation around it &mdash;
        not the other way around.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>The research partner owns identification</h3>
      <p>
        A J-PAL regional office or a comparable academic partner runs the
        randomization, pre-analysis plan, and impact estimation. AVE-RD
        provides the protocol, the instruments, and the analysis code as
        a starting kit.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>An evaluation agency owns the data</h3>
      <p>
        A national evaluation body (in DR's case, IDEICE) hosts the panel
        and brokers the link to administrative records. This is the role
        most often missing in adaptations &mdash; without it, the
        long-run earnings analysis is not feasible.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
</ol>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">Six-month launch</div>
  <h2>What you can stand up in two semesters</h2>
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Months 1&ndash;2.</strong> Wage-data review, partner agreements, IRB submission.</li>
  <li><strong>Months 2&ndash;3.</strong> Video re-shoot or localization, classroom-protocol translation, pilot in 5&ndash;10 schools.</li>
  <li><strong>Month 4.</strong> Sample frame, randomization, baseline survey instrument adapted.</li>
  <li><strong>Months 5&ndash;6.</strong> Field rollout in the first cohort. The full <span class="font-osf">600</span>-school target is a year-two milestone, not a year-one one.</li>
</ul>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">What goes wrong</div>
  <h2>Three failure modes adapters consistently hit</h2>
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Wage data too noisy or too old.</strong> The informative arm depends on credible, recent earnings data by completed schooling level. If the ministry's labor-force data is more than three years stale, the persuasive arm is the more honest test.</li>
  <li><strong>Implementation drift.</strong> The 15-minute classroom protocol is short for a reason. Letting it expand to 30+ minutes loses comparability with the Dominican results.</li>
  <li><strong>No follow-up plan.</strong> AVE-RD's headline test is a long-run one. Adaptations that fund only the first wave foreclose the most valuable measurement five years later.</li>
</ul>
