<p class="dropcap">
  Field operations for AVE-RD's long-run follow-up wave restarted in
  September <span class="font-osf">2025</span>, ten years after the
  original cohort first saw the videos in their grade-7 classrooms. The
  field team is staging out of the same project office in Santo Domingo
  and using the same regional DOP coordinator network that ran the
  <span class="font-osf">2015&ndash;2016</span> waves &mdash;
  institutional memory we said in the funder brief was the cheapest
  research input we had.
</p>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">What's happening now</div>
  <h2>Three pilot regions, then national rollout</h2>
</div>

<p>
  The first six weeks are a contact-tracing pilot in three educational
  regions chosen for their mix of urban, rural, and migration
  characteristics. The team is building the contact-tracing protocol
  against three signals: MINERD enrolment records, the
  Tesorer&iacute;a de la Seguridad Social earnings panel via IDEICE,
  and a directory-assistance step that targets the
  <span class="font-osf">~10</span>% of the cohort estimated to have
  moved abroad.
</p>

<p>
  After the pilot, the field operation expands to the full
  <span class="font-osf">18</span> educational regions on a rolling
  schedule that completes in <span class="font-osf">2026</span>.
</p>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">What we're tracking</div>
  <h2>Pre-registered outcomes</h2>
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Enrolment and completion.</strong> Secondary completion, post-secondary enrolment, vocational versus academic track.</li>
  <li><strong>Earnings.</strong> Formal-sector earnings via TSS, with a self-reported informal-sector module on the survey to bound under-counting.</li>
  <li><strong>Beliefs.</strong> A re-elicitation of the wage-by-schooling-level beliefs from the original baseline, allowing a within-person comparison.</li>
  <li><strong>Migration.</strong> Within-country and international, with a contact-tracing module for those who have moved.</li>
</ul>
