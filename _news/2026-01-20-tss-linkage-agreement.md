<p class="dropcap">
  IDEICE and the Tesorer&iacute;a de la Seguridad Social signed the
  data-handling and de-identification agreement that lets AVE-RD link
  its <span class="font-osf">43,000</span>-student panel to
  formal-sector earnings records. The agreement was the precondition
  for the long-run impact analysis described in the
  <a href="/briefs/why-follow-up/">funder brief</a>. With it now in
  place, the linked dataset is on track to be ready in late
  <span class="font-osf">2026</span>.
</p>

<div class="section-header reveal">
  <div class="eyebrow eyebrow--rule">What changes</div>
  <h2>Three immediate consequences</h2>
</div>

<ol class="numbered-list reveal">
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>The follow-up's headline outcome is now measurable.</h3>
      <p>
        Formal-sector earnings by completed schooling level, linked to
        the original cohort assignment, were the headline number we
        committed to in the pre-analysis plan. The agreement closes the
        last operational gap.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>The replication kit gains a real benchmark.</h3>
      <p>
        With TSS-linked outcomes in hand, the open replication kit can
        report effect sizes in the same units other countries' programs
        report theirs &mdash; not standardized test scores, but local
        currency earnings per year of schooling.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
  <li>
    <div>
      <h3>The fundraise narrows.</h3>
      <p>
        The remaining funding gap is now concentrated in field
        operations and the brief series &mdash; the data-infrastructure
        line is covered. See the
        <a href="/projects/follow-up/">follow-up plan</a> for the
        revised budget.
      </p>
    </div>
  </li>
</ol>
